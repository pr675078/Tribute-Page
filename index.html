Sir Ratan Tata

The man who transformed industries and uplifted millions

Here's the time line of Sir Ratan Tata's life:

  • Ratan Tata is born to Soonoo and Naval Tata.
  • Leaves for Cornell University (Ithaca, New York, USA) at age 17; goes on to study architecture and engineering over a seven-year period
  • Awarded bachelor of architecture degree on the same year join Tata groups.
  • Sir Ratan Tata returned to India and joined Tata group's Empress Mills in Nagpur
  • Is appointed Chairman of Tata Industries; begins the process of transforming it into a promoter of high-technology businesses.
  • Serves as Chairman of Air India, the national carrier.
  • Begins restructuring of the Tata group at a time when the liberalisation of the Indian economy is underway.
  • The growth and globalisation drive of the Tata group gathers pace under his stewardship and the new millennium sees a string of high-profile Tata acquisitions, among them Tetley, Corus, Jaguar Land Rover, Brunner Mond, General Chemical Industrial Products and Daewoo.
  • Launches the Tata Nano, born of the trailblazing small car project he guided and commanded with zeal and determination.
  • The Tata Hall at Harvard University is completed and named in honour of Ratan Tata.
  • Began investing in startups, at a time when India’s startup ecosystem was still emerging.
  • Inducted into the Automotive Hall of Fame, recognising Mr Tata’s quest to produce the country’s first fully indigenous car, the Indica, in 1998, following it with the ground‑breaking Nano in 2008.
  • Set up the Ratan Tata Endowment Foundation.
  • Mr Tata passes away at the age of 86
